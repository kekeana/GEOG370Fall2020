Homework 10: Geoprocessed Earthquakes and Fault Lines Map

Keana Oldham

The following map displays where earthquakes have been recorded in 2019 and where the active fault zones are as of 2014. There is a correlation between where the fault lines occur and where the earthquakes occur.
Research Question: What earthquakes lie within fault zones (area within 500 meters of line)? What counties have the most severe earthquakes and the most fault lines?


Process:
I first dissolved the counties of California, and then made a buffer around this dissolved layer. This California buffer is buffered by 100 km, which is the range large earthquakes can be felt. I then clipped both my fault lines and earthquakes vector layers to reduce the data outside of California. I then buffered both of these clipped files in order to define the fault zone within 500 meters of the fault lines and a circle of radius 1 kilometer from the earthquake epicenter. I created a symmetrical difference of the buffered fault zones and buffered earthquakes. Finally, I created an intersection of the buffered fault zones and buffered earthquakes in order to show where there are earthquakes over fault zones.
